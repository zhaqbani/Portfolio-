Who am I?

### **Who am I?** I am from **Paktia, Afghanistan**, and grew up in a place where few girls were encouraged to study.
I became the **first girl in my village to complete high school**, and soon, I will be the first to enter university. Education, for me, has never been just about achievement—it has been a way to open doors that were once closed to girls like me. I am **curious and calm by nature**, someone who learns best through doing. As the **Vice President of my school’s Computer Club**,
I discovered how much I enjoy problem-solving and creating things that work. Helping organize coding workshops and guiding students through their first projects made me realize how powerful technology can be when shared. Outside the classroom, I stay active through **running, jogging, and gym training**, and once took part in a **marathon**—a reminder that endurance builds both strength and confidence. When I began exploring online education, I saw how many Afghan girls faced the same confusion I once had about digital learning. To change that, I started an initiative that helped more than **10,000 girls access free Coursera courses**, with over **300 completing several courses each**. What began as a small idea became a national network of learners helping one another succeed. I plan to **study Computer Science and Information Technology abroad**, focusing on how technology can address real issues faced by Afghan women. My goal is to develop a **mobile app in Dari and Pashto** that helps women **track menstrual health, access childbirth information, and connect with doctors** in rural areas. For me, success means being **independent enough to empower others**. Every project I build is a step toward that vision.
